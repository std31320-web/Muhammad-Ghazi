# Econometric Analysis: Mineral Export Deficit (MXD) & Current Account Impact

## 🎯 The Problem
Quantifying the precise long-run and short-run dynamics of specific sector gaps on national current account balances is highly complex. In this research, the **Mineral Export Deficit (MXD)**—defined as mineral exports minus mineral imports—was analyzed to understand how structural shifts in resource trade impact macro-level stability. Traditional OLS models fail to account for non-stationary variables and cointegrating relationships in macroeconomic time-series data.

## 🛠️ The Technical Action
* Constructed a rigorous time-series dataset capturing key macroeconomic variables, centering on the Asset Price Monetary Transmission Mechanism.
* Utilized **EViews** to run comprehensive unit root diagnostic testing (ADF and PP tests) to confirm variable integration levels.
* Applied an **Autoregressive Distributed Lag (ARDL)** model structure alongside **Vector Error Correction Modeling (VECM)** to isolate long-run cointegrating vectors and short-run adjustment speeds.
* Implemented strict residual diagnostic tests, checking for heteroskedasticity, serial correlation, and structural stability (CUSUM/CUSUMsq charts).

## 🚀 The Result
The model successfully isolated the structural impact of resource gaps: **the negative coefficient on the MXD variable conclusively proved that an expansion in the mineral export deficit severely hurts the national current account balance over the long run.** The VECM framework precisely calculated the annual speed of adjustment back to equilibrium following a macroeconomic shock.
